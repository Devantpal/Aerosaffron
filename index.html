🌱 The Technology

What is Aeroponics?

A soil-less cultivation method where plant roots are suspended in air and nourished through a fine nutrient mist — enabling unprecedented growth efficiency and precision control.

🌿
🌬️
Air Roots
💧
Mist Feed
📡
IoT Control
🌬️
Superior Oxygen Supply
Roots suspended in air receive 3× more oxygen than soil-based systems, accelerating nutrient absorption and metabolic activity in saffron corms.
💧
95% Water Efficiency
Closed-loop nutrient misting uses up to 95% less water than conventional farming, with zero runoff, zero soil contamination, and full recirculation.
⚡
Faster Growth Cycles
Aeroponic plants grow 30–50% faster due to optimized nutrient delivery, controlled microclimate, and elimination of soil-borne stress factors.
🎯
Precision IoT Control
Real-time sensors monitor temperature, humidity, light, and water levels — ESP32 automatically reacts to any environmental deviation without manual intervention.
🌸 Why Saffron?
💰
Most Expensive Spice
Saffron commands ₹3–5 lakh/kg globally. Even a small indoor unit generating 100g/year creates ₹30,000–50,000 revenue. India exports to 40+ countries.
🌡️
Highly Climate-Sensitive
Requires precisely 23–27°C during growth and ~17°C for corm development. Traditional farming in Kashmir is entirely dependent on unpredictable weather patterns.
🏙️
Urban Farming Potential

📊 Research Outcomes

Results & Findings

Key outcomes from the research prototype presented at ICEICM 2026 — validated against published saffron cultivation literature.

Stable microclimate achieved — Temperature maintained within ±1°C of setpoint using Peltier cooling inside an insulated ice-box enclosure.
Real-time IoT monitoring — ESP32 synced sensor data to Firebase with sub-second latency; dashboard reflected live values without manual refresh.
Automated control loop — System independently managed pump, fan, LED, and cooling based on threshold rules — faster response than manual methods.
Healthy growth conditions — Controlled humidity and light cycle recreated ideal saffron germination environment, confirmed against literature benchmarks (23–27°C).
Low power consumption — Compact Peltier + relay-controlled design consumed significantly less energy than traditional greenhouse HVAC systems.
Cost-effective alternative — Total hardware cost is a fraction of conventional greenhouse setups; suitable for small-scale urban farming at entry-level investment.
